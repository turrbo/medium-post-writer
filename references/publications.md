## Better Programming

**Focus:** Software engineering, development practices, tools, career
**Tone:** Technical but accessible. Show don't lecture
**Sweet Spot:** 5-8 minute reads with code examples
**What editors want:** Practical, real-world experience, not theoretical

### What Works
- "I refactored our entire codebase and here's what broke"
- Honest post-mortems of technical decisions
- Tool comparisons grounded in actual use (not feature lists)
- Contrarian takes on popular frameworks backed by experience
- Specific debugging stories with the actual solution

### What Doesn't Work
- "Introduction to [basic concept]" rehashes
- Feature announcement rewrites
- "Why you should use [framework]" without real tradeoffs
- Theoretical architectural discussions without implementation experience
- Tutorial format without acknowledging what goes wrong

### Style Notes
- Code snippets must be functional and tested
- Conversational but not sloppy
- Self-deprecating humor about bad code is welcome
- Admitting you don't know something is respected
- "Here's what I'd do differently" > "Here's the right way"

## Towards Data Science

**Focus:** Data science, ML, AI, analytics, statistics
**Tone:** Educational but personal. Mix rigor with accessibility
**Sweet Spot:** 6-10 minute reads. Deeper is OK if engaging
**What editors want:** Original analysis, practical tutorials, honest reflections

### What Works
- Novel applications of known techniques
- "I tried X on a real dataset and it failed because..."
- Demystifying concepts without dumbing them down
- Visualizations that reveal something unexpected
- Comparing techniques on YOUR actual problem

### What Doesn't Work
- Regurgitated textbook explanations
- "What is Machine Learning?" entry-level content
- Pure math without practical context
- Hype about AI capabilities without evidence
- Tutorials that work on toy data but not real data

### Style Notes
- Show your work, including failures
- Visualizations should be clear and tell a story
- Link to code/notebooks but don't just dump code
- Acknowledge limitations of your approach
- "This didn't work" is more valuable than "this worked perfectly"

## The Startup

**Focus:** Entrepreneurship, startup culture, business building
**Tone:** Honest, founder-to-founder. Battle-tested advice
**Sweet Spot:** 5-7 minute reads
**What editors want:** Real stories, specific numbers, honest failures

### What Works
- Revenue/growth numbers with context
- "What I wish I knew before raising"
- Specific mistakes with cost attached ("That hire cost us $200K and 6 months")
- Anti-hustle-culture takes grounded in experience
- The unglamorous reality behind the launch

### What Doesn't Work
- Motivational platitudes ("just believe in yourself")
- "5 tips for startup success" listicle energy
- Fundraising announcements disguised as articles
- Advice from people who haven't built anything
- "I made $X in Y days" without the ugly middle

### Style Notes
- Numbers build trust. Include them
- Vulnerability + specific lessons > empty inspiration
- "I failed at this" resonates more than "I succeeded at this"
- Name the specific mistake, not just the category
- Readers here have been burned by generic advice

## UX Collective

**Focus:** Design, UX research, product design, design systems
**Tone:** Thoughtful, visually aware, design-literate
**Sweet Spot:** 5-8 minutes with screenshots/mockups
**What editors want:** Original design thinking, case studies, process transparency

### What Works
- Case studies showing the messy design process (not just the final mockup)
- Critiques of popular products' UX decisions
- Design system evolution stories
- Research findings that challenged assumptions
- Accessibility deep dives with real examples

### What Doesn't Work
- "UX 101" basics
- Dribbble-pretty mockups without the thinking behind them
- "Redesigning [major app]" without user research
- Tool comparisons that are just feature lists
- Trend roundups without critical analysis

### Style Notes
- Show process, not just outcomes
- Before/after with reasoning
- User quotes and research data add credibility
- Admit when your design hypothesis was wrong
- Visual examples are expected, not optional

## Better Humans

**Focus:** Self-improvement, habits, psychology, wellness
**Tone:** Evidence-based but personal. Not preachy
**Sweet Spot:** 5-7 minutes
**What editors want:** Personal experience backed by science, not the reverse

### What Works
- "I tried [method] for 30 days and here's the honest result"
- Psychology insights applied to specific personal situations
- Habit changes that actually stuck (and why others didn't)
- Mental health honesty without toxic positivity
- Small, specific behavioral changes with real outcomes

### What Doesn't Work
- "5 morning habits of successful people"
- Repackaged self-help book summaries
- "Just meditate" advice without the struggle
- Productivity optimization without acknowledging the emotional underneath
- Perfect transformation arcs (struggle -> system -> success)

### Style Notes
- Lead with the personal, bring in the research second
- Admit when a "good habit" didn't work for you
- The mess is the story. The system is secondary
- "Still working on it" is a valid conclusion
- Link to actual studies, not just books

## OneZero / Future Human

**Focus:** Technology's impact on society, digital culture, future
**Tone:** Journalistic but opinionated. Long-form analysis
**Sweet Spot:** 7-12 minutes. Depth is expected
**What editors want:** Original reporting, analysis, cultural criticism

### What Works
- "Here's what's actually happening with [tech trend]"
- Reporting on lesser-known consequences of popular technology
- Cultural criticism of tech industry norms
- First-person investigations
- Historical context for current tech debates

### What Doesn't Work
- Hot takes without research
- "AI will change everything" without specifics
- Silicon Valley cheerleading
- Rehashed news commentary
- Surface-level trend analysis

## Personal Growth / Self-Published

**Focus:** Any topic. No editorial filter
**Tone:** Whatever is authentic to YOU
**Sweet Spot:** 4-7 minutes for building audience
**What works:** Your genuine voice without publication constraints

### The Self-Pub Advantage
- No editorial compromises
- Full control over voice and structure
- Can be more experimental
- More personal and vulnerable content
- No pitch process

### The Self-Pub Risk
- No built-in distribution
- No editorial quality check
- Easy to be self-indulgent without feedback
- Harder to get initial traction

## Level Up Coding

**Focus:** Programming tutorials, career advice, coding culture
**Tone:** Friendly, approachable, practical
**Sweet Spot:** 5-7 minutes with code examples
**What editors want:** Practical guides for working developers

### What Works
- Real problems you solved with specific code
- Career transitions with honest timelines
- "Things I wish I knew as a junior developer" (with specifics)
- Comparison articles grounded in actual project use
- Debugging war stories

### What Doesn't Work
- Beginner tutorials for well-documented topics
- "Learn X in Y minutes" without depth
- Pure theory without application

## The Writing Cooperative

**Focus:** Craft of writing, publishing, creative process
**Tone:** Writerly but unpretentious
**Sweet Spot:** 4-6 minutes
**What editors want:** Honest writing about writing

### What Works
- Process transparency (how you actually write, not how you should)
- Rejection stories with what you learned
- Specific craft observations from your own reading
- Publishing industry realities
- "What I've gotten wrong about writing"

## General Publication Strategy

### Before Submitting
1. Read 10+ recent articles in the publication
2. Note the length, tone, and structure that gets featured
3. Check if they accept pitches or full drafts
4. Read submission guidelines (every publication has them)
5. Follow the editors on X/Medium

### Pitch Do's
- Reference a specific article they published and why yours complements it
- Be concise: topic, angle, why you're the right person to write it
- Include a hook paragraph (not the full draft)

### Pitch Don'ts
- "I have a great article about [vague topic]"
- Sending the same pitch to 10 publications
- Ignoring their stated submission process
- Following up more than once after 2 weeks

### Tag Strategy
- Use 5 tags maximum
- Mix broad (Technology, Startup) with specific (Remote Work, SaaS)
- Check which tags have active readership
- First tag matters most for distribution
