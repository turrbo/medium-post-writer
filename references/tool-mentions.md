# Tool/Product Mention Guidelines (Medium Edition)

Only mention tools if user requests it. Default is NO tool mentions.

---

## Key Difference from Reddit and X.com

Medium articles are long-form. This gives you more room to bury a tool mention naturally -- but also more room for it to feel promotional if handled poorly. The longer format means readers have more time to develop trust before encountering a mention, but also more context to judge whether the article exists to help them or to sell them something.

---

## Subtlety Levels

### Level 1-2 (Barely There)
"A friend helped me set up some automation. I don't fully understand it but it basically pulls my scattered notes into one place every morning. That alone changed things."
- Tool might not be named at all
- Feels like a minor detail in a larger story
- The automation is not the point -- the behavior change is

### Level 3-4 (Brief Mention)
"I ended up using [tool] for this, mostly because my friend already had it set up. It's fine -- it does the thing. The real change was that I started looking at my notes first thing instead of email."
- Named once, casually
- "It's fine" is perfect -- not excited, not dismissive
- Immediately pivots back to the human insight

### Level 5-6 (Noticeable but Not Dominant)
"After trying three or four different apps -- none of which I stuck with for more than a week -- I landed on [tool]. It's not perfect. It crashes sometimes and the UI is kind of ugly. But it does the one thing I actually need: it shows me what I was working on yesterday. That's it. That's the whole thing."
- More detail, but grounded in honest experience
- Specific limitations (crashes, ugly UI)
- The value is deliberately small and specific
- Paragraph still works if you remove the tool name

### Level 7-10 (Don't Use -- Too Promotional)
Reads like a sponsored post. Medium readers are extremely sensitive to this. Will damage credibility for the entire article and future articles.

---

## Placement Rules

### Where to Put It:
- **Deep in the article** (40-70% through)
- **After establishing the real insight** (the human story comes first)
- **Inside a paragraph about something else** (tool is an aside, not a topic)
- **As one option among several** ("I tried X, then Y, then landed on [tool]")

### Where NOT to Put It:
- The opening (colors the entire article as promotional)
- As the turning point of the story ("then I found [tool] and everything changed")
- In a subheading ("How [Tool] Fixed My Productivity")
- In the conclusion (feels like a CTA)
- More than once (repetition = ad)

---

## Language Patterns

### Phrases That Work:
- "I ended up using [tool], mostly because..."
- "A friend set me up with [tool]"
- "I think it was [tool]? Something like that"
- "[tool] handles the boring part"
- "It's not great but it works"

### Phrases That DON'T Work:
- "I discovered [tool] and it changed everything"
- "[tool] is the best [category] I've found"
- "If you're struggling with X, try [tool]"
- "[tool] allows you to seamlessly..."
- "Full disclosure: I use [tool]" (paradoxically makes it sound MORE like an ad)

---

## Required Disclaimers

Include at least one of these near the mention:
- "It's not perfect"
- "I don't fully understand how it works"
- "I've only been using it for [short time]"
- "It breaks sometimes"
- "Could probably do this with a spreadsheet honestly"
- "Not sure it's the tool or just the habit"

---

## Promotion Test

**Critical:** Remove every mention of the tool from the article. Read it again. Does the article:
1. Still tell a complete story?
2. Still provide value?
3. Still make the same point?

If the answer to any of these is NO, the article is too promotional. Rewrite with the story first, tool mention optional.

---

## Medium-Specific Risks

- **Credibility erosion:** One obviously sponsored article makes readers question every future recommendation
- **Publication rejection:** Most editors will reject articles that feel promotional
- **Comment section:** Medium readers WILL call out promotional content in responses
- **Algorithmic penalty:** Consistently promotional content gets reduced distribution
- **Partner Program risk:** Articles flagged as marketing may be excluded from earnings

The goal: A reader finishes the article thinking about the insight, not the tool. If they remember the tool at all, it should be "oh yeah, they mentioned some app" not "that article was basically an ad for [tool]."
